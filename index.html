FAQ

The crankset I want doesn't come with the right spindle!

You can purchase the correct spindle from our spare parts page.

What is BB Type?

BB Type refers to the spindle diameter of the crankset. 24mm cranks include Aeffect, Aeffect R, Ride. 30mm cranks include Atlas, Era, Turbine.

What is "DH"?

DH refers to the spindle type. DH spindles have longer splines and are only compatible with Atlas Cranks.

I see multiple spindle options — which should I pick?

You might see multiple spindle lengths that work for your setup. Most people prefer the shortest possible spindle to maintain a natural Q-Factor, but others prefer a wider stance. Always double-check your minimum Q-Factor for your frame. See the linked doc for measurements: Q-Factors & Chainlines.

What is chainline? How do I find out?

Many brands provide chainline specs on their websites, but for others you may need to contact them directly. There is some correlation between your bike's hub spacing and chainline, but it’s not always exact. Park Tool has a great video explaining chainline: Watch here. We also have a chart available to estimate it, but you should confirm your measurements before buying.
